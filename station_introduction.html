The Five Stations

Rotate through each station. Spend 15 minutes at each.

STATION 1

Mood Passage Analysis

Compare paired passages from both texts. How does Doyle create suspense ("What happened?") while Gaiman creates dread ("I fear what I'm about to understand")?
STATION 2

The Unreliable Narrator

Now that you know the twist, re-read key excerpts from Gaiman. What clues were hidden in plain sight? What did the narrator choose not to tell us?

STATION 3

Setting as Character

Both stories use Victorian London‚Äîbut the city does different work. In Doyle's world, observation leads to truth. In Gaiman's, truth might destroy you.

STATION 4

Sympathy Flip

Genre conventions teach us: detectives are heroes. Gaiman flips this. Analyze how the story manipulates our sympathies by using familiar roles.

STATION 5

Genre Mashup Pitch

Design your own Sherlock Holmes remix! Choose a genre (sci-fi, romance, Western, etc.) and plan how Holmes would work within it. Pitch to your group.
