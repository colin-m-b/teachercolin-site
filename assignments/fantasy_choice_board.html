üìã What You'll Create

üìã What You'll Create

üë®‚Äçüë©‚Äçüëß Tips for Parents

This summative assessment allows students to demonstrate their understanding of two fantasy novels through their chosen format. Here's how you can support your student effectively:

‚úÖ Helpful Ways to Support

  • Discuss the books together: Ask your student to explain the plot, characters, and themes to you. Teaching someone else deepens understanding.
  • Help them choose wisely: Encourage your student to pick the option that genuinely interests them, not just the one that seems "easiest."
  • Ask guiding questions: "What evidence from the book supports that idea?" or "Why do you think the author made that choice?"
  • Provide structure: Help your student break the 240 minutes into manageable chunks with clear goals for each work session.
  • Encourage planning first: The planning worksheet exists for a reason‚Äîcompleting it thoroughly makes the final product much stronger.

"Can you explain to me how Aslan's sacrifice connects to Christian symbolism?" or "What makes Percy similar to Edmund?" are great conversation starters that help students articulate their thinking.

üö´ What to Avoid

  • Don't write or create for them: Even with good intentions, this prevents your student from learning and developing their own ideas.
  • Don't provide your interpretations: Ask questions that help them find their own answers rather than telling them what themes to focus on.
  • Don't prioritize polish over thinking: A thoughtful argument with simple presentation beats a beautiful product with shallow analysis.
