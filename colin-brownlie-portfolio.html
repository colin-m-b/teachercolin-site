Portfolio Overview

Hello and welcome to my teaching portfolio. Here you can see samples of my work as a teacher and as an instructional coach. Student Work

Student-Created Magazine

As I mentioned in the section above, this year my grade 7 class learned about biography as a genre, exploring common features of the form and how writers can make biographical writing more interesting than just a recitation of facts.

After reading short biographies and thinking about how this genre works, the students were tasked with writing their own biographies for a magazine the class put together. The process of assembling the magazine was wonderful to watch - the class had to work together, take on the various jobs at a magazine, such as editing, cover design, page layout, etc. You can see a sample of that work here - with pictures and student names removed, of course.

Teaching Philosophy

As a teacher and an instructional coach, I work to find the right mix of direct instruction and inquiry-based learning. This mix changes from person to person and from student to student; that's one reason this profession never gets stale. We need to meet everyone where they're at to help them move forward (to greatly simplify the work of Lev Vygotsky), so we have to look at people as individual learners as much as possible. It's not easy, and no one is perfect at it, but then again, if it was easy, what fun would that be?

So how is it done? Its starts with building a classroom culture that embraces making mistakes. After all, that's how we learn new things - we try, we fail, we do it better next time. We build routines that reduce cognitive load. We discuss and share perspectives. We review and play games. We take what we've learned and apply it to new things in ways that are difficult. We celebrate our successes and we struggle through our challenges knowing that it's supposed to be hard. In my classroom, that's how we learn.
