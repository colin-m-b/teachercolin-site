Suspense vs. Dread: How Authors Create Atmosphere

Mystery creates suspense: "What happened? Can we solve it?"
Horror creates dread: "I fear what I'm about to understand."

How do Doyle and Gaiman use language differently to achieve these effects?

How to Analyze These Passages

  1. Read each paired set of passages carefully‚Äîthey describe similar situations
  2. Use your worksheet to annotate: What word choices stand out? What information is given vs. withheld?
  3. Consider: What does each narrator want you to feel? How do you know?
  4. Record your observations using the guiding questions on your worksheet

Look for in Doyle (Mystery):

  • Precise, observable details
  • Logical sequence of events
  • Clues the reader can follow
  • Building toward revelation

Look for in Gaiman (Horror):

  • Sensory overload or confusion
  • What the narrator avoids saying
  • Physical/emotional reactions
  • The unknowable or incomprehensible
